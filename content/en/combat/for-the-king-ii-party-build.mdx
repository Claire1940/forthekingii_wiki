<Section icon={Map} title="Fundamentals of a For The King II Party Build">

Creating a high-performance **for the king ii party build** requires more than just picking your favorite characters. In this turn-based roguelite, the synergy between classes, stats, and grid positioning determines your longevity. A party consists of four slots, and failing to fill specific roles like "Tank" or "Healer" will lead to a rapid collapse during dungeon crawls or boss encounters.

| Role | Primary Objective | Key Stats | Recommended Classes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Frontline** | Absorb damage, Guard allies | Vitality, Strength | Blacksmith, Fallen Knight |
| **Physical DPS** | Single-target burst, Ambush | Awareness | Hunter, Angler, Archer |
| **Magic DPS** | AOE damage, Status effects | Intelligence | Scholar, Alchemist |
| **Support** | Party healing, Herb economy | Intelligence, Talent | Herbalist, Monk, Busker |

<Callout type="info" title="Positioning Matters">
The Battle Grid is a 2x4 space. Always place your highest Vitality characters in the front row to trigger the **Guard** mechanic, which allows them to intercept physical attacks meant for the backline.
</Callout>

In 2026, the meta emphasizes flexibility. While a specialized party can excel in specific biomes, a generalist build is safer for long-form campaigns. You must ensure your party can handle both Physical Defense and Magical Resistance, as enemies often alternate between high-armor and high-ward configurations.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Class Synergies and Stat Optimization">

A successful **for the king ii party build** thrives on how well your gear matches your character's primary stats. In For The King II, weapons use specific stats for their hit rolls. If you give a Strength-based hammer to a Scholar, they will miss almost every swing, wasting precious turns and Focus.

<Callout type="tip" title="Focus Recovery Synergy">
The **Scholar** possesses the **Refocus** ability, which can restore Focus to themselves at the end of a turn. Pair this with a **Herbalist** who finds herbs to ensure your party never runs dry on resources during long dungeon runs.
</Callout>
