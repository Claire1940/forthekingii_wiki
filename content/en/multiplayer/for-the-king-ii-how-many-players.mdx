<Section icon={Map} title="How to Set Up Your Multiplayer Lobby">

Setting up a session is straightforward, but there are a few nuances to ensure everyone connects properly. If you are the host, you are responsible for managing the "Lore Store" unlocks and the initial "House Rules" that define the difficulty of your run. 

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Create or Load an Adventure">
    From the main menu, select **Multiplayer**. You can either start a "New Adventure" or "Load Game." If loading, ensure all previous players are ready to rejoin, as you cannot easily swap players mid-session without restarting the lobby.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure Lobby Settings">
    Set your **Room Name** and **Password**. Choose your server region (US East, Europe, etc.) based on where the majority of your players are located to minimize latency during turn transitions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Invite Your Party">
    Use the platform's overlay (Shift+Tab on Steam) to invite friends directly, or give them the Room Name and Password to search for in the **Server Browser**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Assign Character Slots">
    Once everyone is in the lobby, the host can assign which player controls which hero slot. You can mix and match; for example, one player can control two heroes while two others control one each.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Session Tip">
If a player disconnects during a run, the host should save the game and return to the main menu. Re-hosting the lobby and having the player rejoin is the most reliable way to restore their control over their character.
</Callout>

</Section>
