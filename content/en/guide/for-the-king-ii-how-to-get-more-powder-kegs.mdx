<Section icon={Target} title="Primary Acquisition Methods for Powder Kegs">

To consistently **get more powder kegs**, you must engage with the game's economic and exploration systems. There is no single "infinite" source, but by rotating through different methods, you can keep your inventory stocked for difficult boss encounters.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Scour Town Marketplaces">
    Towns are your most reliable source for consumables. Each biome contains several settlements where the **Marketplace** stock is randomized. **Powder kegs** typically appear in the **Gear** or **Consumables** tab. If a town does not have them, check the next nearest settlement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete Side Quests">
    When visiting a town, always check the **Quest Board**. Look for tasks that list "Item Rewards" or "Supply Crate" as a prize. High-difficulty quests often yield rare explosives and high-tier **Godsbeard**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Defeat Specific Enemy Archetypes">
    Farming specific mobs is a viable way to **get more powder kegs**. Enemies that utilize mechanical constructs or black powder (such as **Gunners**, **Pirates**, and **Clockwork Soldiers**) have a higher probability of dropping explosives upon death.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utilize the Alchemist's Passive">
    If you have unlocked the **Alchemist** class, their **Find Potion** ability can occasionally trigger to provide bombs or similar explosive items during overworld travel, effectively giving you free resources.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Strategic Combat Application">

Simply having a **powder keg** is not enough; you must know when to deploy it. Because these items hit a full row, they are best saved for encounters where the enemy formation is "clumped." Using a keg on a single target is a waste of a high-value resource.

**Tactical Considerations:**
- **Target Priority**: Use kegs to clear out fragile back-row supports or glass-cannon casters.
- **Status Synergy**: Pair explosives with **Frozen** or **Dazed** status effects to maximize damage or prevent enemy retaliation.
- **Grid Control**: If enemies move to a single column, use column-based attacks instead and save your **powder kegs** for row-based formations.
