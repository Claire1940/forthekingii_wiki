<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mastery: The Battle Grid and Focus">

Combat in *For The King II* has evolved into a tactical chess match. The 2x4 grid allows for sophisticated positioning. Front-row characters can utilize the "Guard" ability to absorb damage intended for the back row, while certain weapons can hit entire rows or columns.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Positioning">
    Place your Blacksmith or high-Vitality character in the front-center. Keep your Scholar and Herbalist in the back corners to minimize exposure to splash damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze Enemy Intents">
    Hover over enemies to see their targeting reticle. If an enemy is preparing a column attack, move your characters to different vertical lanes during their secondary action.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Focus Allocation">
    Use Focus points to guarantee hits on high-priority targets (like healers) or to ensure a successful "Stun" or "Freeze" proc that can skip an enemy's turn.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secondary Actions">
    Don't forget secondary actions. Use them to consume herbs, swap equipment, or move one tile on the grid before committing to your primary attack.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Status effects are more lethal than ever in 2026. A single "Acid" proc can permanently destroy a legendary weapon, while "Daze" can rob you of your crucial secondary actions.

| Status Effect | Impact | Recommended Cure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bleed** | Damage over time every turn | Bandages / Godsbeard |
| **Shock** | Automatic failure on the next roll | Panax / Focus |
| **Acid** | Chance to dissolve armor/weapons | Panax (Immediate) |
| **Daze** | Removes secondary action | Panax / Wait it out |
| **Frozen** | Target takes significantly more damage | Fire damage / Panax |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Danger of Acid">
Acid is the most dangerous status effect for long-term survival. If a character is hit with Acid, prioritize curing it with Panax immediately. Losing a high-tier shield or weapon in the middle of a dungeon can end a run.
</Callout>

</Section>
