<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Masterclass: Grid Positioning & Status Effects">

Combat in For The King II takes place on a 2x4 battle grid. This layout makes spatial awareness just as important as your character stats. Your positioning dictates who can be targeted, who can protect allies, and how area-of-effect (AOE) attacks impact your formation.

| Grid Row | Recommended Classes | Primary Tactical Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Front Row** | Blacksmith, Monk, Shield-users | Absorbs physical attacks, blocks melee strikes, and utilizes Guard to protect the backline. |
| **Back Row** | Hunter, Scholar, Herbalist, Alchemist | Safely executes ranged attacks, casts spells, and applies healing without taking direct melee pressure. |

Understanding how AOE shapes function is vital. Row attacks hit all targets horizontally, while column attacks strike vertically. Spacing out your characters prevents a single enemy spell from decimating your entire party.

<Callout type="warning" title="Dreaded Status Conditions">
Status effects can quickly derail a successful run. Always carry specialized cures such as Panax to remove debilitating debuffs before they cause permanent damage.
</Callout>

The overworld and dungeons are filled with enemies capable of inflicting severe status conditions. Knowing what these effects do and how to counter them is the difference between a successful extraction and a total party wipe.

| Status Effect | Combat Penalty | Optimal Countermeasure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dazed** | Disables the target's next secondary action, blocking movement and item use. | Cleanse immediately with Panax or use abilities that grant extra actions. |
| **Stunned** | Forces the affected character to completely skip their next turn. | Prevent by equipping stun-resistant gear or focus-firing stun-capable enemies. |
| **Acid** | Dissolves equipped weapons or armor permanently over time. | Prioritize killing Acid-inflicting enemies first; carry backup equipment. |
| **Bleed** | Inflicts physical damage over time at the start of every combat turn. | Apply bandages or use a dedicated party heal to out-sustain the damage. |

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Campaign Walkthrough: Chapter Pacing & Quest Management">

The main campaign spans seven narratively linked adventures, presenting an escalating challenge that punishes inefficient play. To survive the rising Chaos levels and complete your objectives, you must establish a structured gameplay loop.

Managing the overworld map requires a careful balance between exploring for resources and rushing the main story objectives. If you spend too many turns wandering aimlessly, the Chaos meter will rise, strengthening enemies and making the environment increasingly hostile.

  <Step num="2" title="Scout the Biome Thoroughly">
