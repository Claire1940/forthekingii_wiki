<Section icon={Target} title="The Loadout and Trait System">

One of the significant upgrades in this sequel is the introduction of Loadouts. Before starting a new campaign, you spend **Loadout Points** to equip your characters with starting weapons, armor, and traits. The number of points available depends on the chosen difficulty level; harder campaigns provide fewer points, forcing you to make tougher strategic decisions.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="World Encounters and Sanctums">

Beyond items and classes, the store allows you to unlock "Grand Sanctums" and unique map locations. These encounters provide powerful, permanent blessings to a single character but are often guarded or hidden in dangerous biomes.

| Sanctum Name | Blessing Effect | Ideal For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sanctum of Blood** | Increases life steal / HP growth | Blacksmith / Woodcutter |
| **Sanctum of Focus** | Increases max focus points | Scholar / Alchemist |
| **Sanctum of Haste** | Permanent speed increase | Pathfinder / Hunter |
| **Sanctum of Wealth** | Bonus gold from all sources | Busker / Bard |
| **Sanctum of Wisdom** | XP gain and intelligence boost | Mage / Scholar |

<Callout type="warning" title="Sanctum Protection">
Sanctums can be destroyed if the character holding the blessing dies. Always prioritize the survival of your "Blessed" character during difficult boss fights.
</Callout>

In addition to Sanctums, you can unlock mercenaries like **Grimshaw** or **Claudia**. These NPCs can be hired at specific locations in Fahrul to bolster your party's numbers, which is particularly useful if you are playing with fewer than four human-controlled characters.
