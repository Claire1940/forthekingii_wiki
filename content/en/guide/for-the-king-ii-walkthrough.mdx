<Section icon={Map} title="For The King II Walkthrough: Step-by-Step Campaign Roadmap">

The core campaign is a challenging journey split into seven narratively linked adventures. Each chapter presents unique biomes, rising Chaos pressure, and deadly dungeons. To survive the journey, you must establish a methodical progression rhythm.

This step-by-step roadmap outlines the essential habits that keep a party alive long enough to turn early overworld chaos into steady progress.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Your Party Roles Early">
    Before launching a new campaign run, ensure your four-player team covers all critical bases. A standard reliable setup includes a Blacksmith on the front row, a Hunter for ranged physical damage, a Scholar for magic control, and an Herbalist for healing. Avoid stacking too many fragile damage dealers on the same team.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spend Focus Where Failure Hurts Most">
    Focus is your most valuable resource. Do not hoard it until your party is on the brink of collapse. Use Focus to guarantee critical attacks, land high-impact crowd control spells, pass urgent overworld movement checks, or secure town quest objectives.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate the Biome Sanctum and Stone Hero">
    Every major biome contains a Sanctum and a Stone Hero. Prioritize scouting the map to find these locations before rushing the main quest objective. Sanctums grant powerful permanent buffs and protect characters from death once, while Stone Heroes provide massive experience boosts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Town Visits and Quests">
    Treat every town as a major power spike. Upon entering a new town, check the shop inventory for fresh weapons, Godsbeard, and Panax. Accept town quests that offer rewards tailored to your party's current needs, and hire mercenaries if you require extra frontline protection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Control Enemy Overworld Pulls">
    Avoid dragging multiple enemy groups into a single battle. Pay close attention to the red enemy radius on the overworld. Use the Hunter's Elite Ambush ability to isolate targets, or regroup your party members closely before engaging in a fight to ensure everyone joins the battle grid.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Distribute Loot Based on Core Stats">
    Never distribute gear randomly. Use the party summary screen to match weapons and armor to the character whose core stats support them. A high-damage awareness bow belongs exclusively to your Hunter, while defensive shields must go directly to your Blacksmith.
  </Step>
  <Step num="7" title="Embrace Roguelite Defeat and Unlocks">
    If your party falls, do not treat the run as wasted time. Use the Lore currency earned during your playthrough to unlock advanced classes, starting loadout items, and powerful traits in the Lore Store. These unlocks significantly ease the difficulty of future campaign attempts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Battle Grid Tactics and Loadout Optimization">

Combat takes place on a dynamic 2x4 battle grid. Your positioning on this grid determines which characters can protect allies, target enemies, or fall victim to area-of-effect (AOE) attacks. Managing your front and back rows is just as important as landing your physical attacks.

| Row | Recommended Classes | Key Functions | Positioning Strategy |
|-----|---------------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Front Row | Blacksmith, Fallen Knight | Absorbs pressure, guards allies | Put durable tanks here |
| Back Row | Scholar, Hunter, Herbalist | Ranged damage, magic, healing | Protect from splash damage |

Area attacks can target entire rows or columns. If you clump your fragile spellcasters together, a single enemy column attack can devastate your backline. Spread your characters out when facing enemies with high magic capabilities, and use your own row-clearing weapons when opponents line up poorly.

Furthermore, status effects can quickly turn a victorious fight into a disaster. Understanding how to counter these effects is essential for deep dungeon runs.

| Status Effect | Combat Impact | Cure or Counter |
|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Dazed | Removes next secondary action | Panax or wait out duration |
| Stunned | Skips target's next turn | Cleanse abilities or wait |
| Burn | Fire damage over time | Water or wait out duration |
| Lightning | Makes next roll fail automatically | Panax or clean status |
| Poison | Damage over time and lower stats | Panax or town healing |
| Acid | Dissolves gear permanently | Protect gear or defeat caster |

<Callout type="success" title="Combat Efficiency">
Always check the enemy's defense type before committing your attacks. Attacking heavily armored targets with physical weapons is highly inefficient. Instead, target their physical defense with magic damage, or use pierce attacks to bypass their armor entirely.
</Callout>

</Section>

For The King II supports solo play, online co-op, and local co-op. However, understanding how platform features and lobby saves work will prevent matchmaking frustrations.

When managing multiplayer campaigns, remember that players cannot join an active game session in progress. The host must save the game, return to the main adventure menu, recreate the lobby, allow all players to connect, and then reload the save file.

<Callout type="info" title="Lobby Troubleshooting">
If a multiplayer lobby does not appear for your friends, verify that all players are running the same game version. Checking your region settings in the matchmaking menu can also resolve connection issues.
