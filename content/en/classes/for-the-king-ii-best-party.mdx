<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Party Positioning">

Properly placing your **for the king ii best party** on the grid is just as important as the classes you choose. A single mistake in positioning can lead to a "wipe" if an enemy uses a column attack that hits both your tank and your healer.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Anchor the Frontline">
    Place your highest Vitality character (usually the Blacksmith) in the front row. Ensure they are equipped with a shield to enable the **Guard** ability, which reduces incoming damage for the ally directly behind them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Shield the Healer">
    Place your Herbalist or primary support character in the back row, directly behind the Guarding tank. This creates a vertical "safe lane" that forces enemies to break through the tank's armor before reaching your healer.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Spread the Damage Dealers">
    Position your DPS units (Hunter, Scholar, or Alchemist) in the remaining tiles. Avoid clumping all four characters in a single 2x2 square if the enemy has "Splash" or "Area" attacks, as this maximizes their damage efficiency against you.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Adjust for AOE Threats">
    If facing enemies with Row attacks, consider moving characters to different horizontal lanes. Use your secondary action to reposition if you see an enemy charging a massive area-of-effect spell.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Movement Mechanics">
Repositioning costs a secondary action. In for the king ii, you should only move if the defensive benefit (avoiding a column hit) outweighs the benefit of using an item or a utility skill during that same turn.
</Callout>

</Section>
