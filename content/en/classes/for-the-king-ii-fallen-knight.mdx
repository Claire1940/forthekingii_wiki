<Section icon={Swords} title="Special Abilities: Knight's Oath and Charge Attack">

The true power of the **for the king ii fallen knight** lies in their unique ability set. These skills allow the Knight to manipulate the timeline and the battle grid, providing utility that goes beyond simple damage.

### Knight's Oath
This is the signature combat mechanic for the class. When activated, the Fallen Knight holds their turn to "charge" a more powerful strike. This is not merely a delay; it transforms the selected weapon skill into an Area of Effect (AoE) version of itself.

| Ability Name | Trigger | Primary Effect | Secondary Buffs |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Knight's Oath** | Combat Menu | Charges turn for AoE attack | Protection / Armor Up |
| **Charge Attack** | Ally at Critical HP | Shields flanking allies | Provides Decoy / Cover |

While charging, the Knight receives defensive boosts, making them an excellent target for enemy "aggro" while they prepare to unleash a devastating blow. If the original target of the charge dies before the Knight acts, the game will automatically retarget to a new primary enemy, ensuring no damage is wasted.

<Callout type="tip" title="Tactical Charging">
Use Knight's Oath when facing groups of three or more enemies. The splash damage generated by the charge often outweighs the damage of two separate standard attacks, especially when combined with high-damage weapons like Lances or Hammers.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Strategy and Positioning">

To maximize the effectiveness of the **for the king ii fallen knight**, you must understand the battle grid. The Knight is most effective when positioned to take advantage of their passive "Decoy" and "Protection" triggers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Positioning">
    Place the Fallen Knight in the center of the front row. This ensures they are adjacent to the maximum number of allies, allowing their passive shielding to trigger if an ally falls to critical health.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Charge Cycle">
    Identify high-priority enemy clusters. Use Knight's Oath on your first turn if the enemies have high health. This prepares your AoE splash for the second round of combat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Focus Utilization">
    Save your Focus points for the "Charge Attack" release. Since the charge takes an entire turn to set up, ensuring the final hit lands (and potentially crits) is vital for party efficiency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Synergy with Support">
    Pair the Knight with a Herbalist or Alchemist. Since the Knight will be taking the brunt of the damage while charging, they need consistent healing or armor buffs from the backline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Battle Grid Tip">
Standing on flanking tiles allows the Fallen Knight to shield allies more effectively. If a teammate is about to die, the Knight will provide "Decoy," drawing the attack toward themselves instead of the weakened party member.
</Callout>

</Section>
