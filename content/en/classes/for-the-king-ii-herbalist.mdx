Their primary role is resource management—specifically keeping the party's HP high while minimizing the consumption of rare herbs like Godsbeard. <Section icon={Sparkles} title="Special Abilities and Passive Skills">

The strength of the **for the king ii herbalist** lies in its unique ability set, which provides both passive resource generation and active combat utility. Understanding the proc rates and limitations of these skills is the difference between a wiped party and a successful run.

### Core Abilities Table

| Ability Name | Type | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Find Herb** | Passive | Chance to find a random herb at the end of a turn (Overworld or Dungeon) |
| **Party Heal** | Active | Heals all adjacent/grouped allies when using a single Godsbeard |

The *Find Herb* ability is a game-changer for long-term sustainability. Because herbs become increasingly expensive in towns as the "Chaos" or "Difficulty" levels rise, having a character that generates them for free is invaluable. *Party Heal* is equally critical; in *For The King II*, it functions best when your party is grouped on the grid, though it can still reach allies across the battlefield depending on the specific encounter mechanics.

In *For The King II*, the strategy for the **for the king ii herbalist** revolves around the new secondary action system. Swapping weapons no longer ends your turn, which allows the Herbalist to adapt to enemy resistances on the fly.

### Healing Optimization Steps

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Assess Party HP">
    Check if multiple allies are below 50% HP. If only one is hurt, consider a single-target heal to save the Party Heal potential.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Positioning Check">
    Ensure the Herbalist is in a tile that provides protection or avoid tiles with active debuffs like "Fire" or "Poison."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Secondary Action Usage">
    Use your secondary action to consume a Pipe-boosted herb or swap to a shield if an enemy is targeting your row.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Party Heal">
    Use the Godsbeard. In FTK2, this will trigger the AOE heal effect, restoring health to the entire group based on your pipe level.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Pipe Upgrades">
The effectiveness of the Herbalist's healing is directly tied to their Pipe level. Prioritize spending gold on Pipe upgrades (up to Level 3) before buying expensive armor.
</Callout>
