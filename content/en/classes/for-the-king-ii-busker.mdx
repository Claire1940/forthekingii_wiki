<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Special Abilities and Passive Skills">

The true value of the **for the king ii busker** lies in their passive abilities. These skills do not require an active turn to use, meaning the Busker provides value simply by existing in the party. Both abilities scale primarily with the **Talent** stat, making it the most important stat for a support-oriented Busker.

| Ability | Type | Effect Description | Scaling Stat |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Distract** | Passive (Combat) | Chance to foil an enemy's ability roll, causing them to fail a slot. | **Talent** |
| **Entertain** | Passive (World) | Gain Gold during Overworld movement or while inside Dungeons. | **Talent** |

**Distract** is one of the most powerful defensive passives in the game. When an enemy attacks, there is a percentage chance (based on your Talent) that one of their action slots will automatically fail. This can turn a "Great Success" attack into a standard hit or cause a complex spell to fail entirely.

**Entertain** provides a steady stream of income. As you move across the map, the Busker "performs" for the local populace. While the individual gold amounts are small, they accumulate quickly, allowing your party to afford better equipment or more Godsbeard than your opponents.

<Callout type="warning" title="Scaling Note">
If you build your Busker purely for Strength, your Distract and Entertain triggers will be significantly less frequent. Balance is key.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Starting Strategy & Progression">

Starting a campaign with the **for the king ii busker** requires a clear plan for your character's role. Because they are unlocked by default, they are often a staple in many beginner compositions.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Loadout">
    The Busker starts with a **Worn Lute**. This is a basic Talent-based weapon. If you intend to play as a frontline tank, look for a Blacksmith's hammer or a basic sword in the first town.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Early Gold Management">
    Use the gold generated by **Entertain** to prioritize Pipe upgrades for your party. Since the Busker generates extra income, they should often be the one to "sponsor" the party's early-game herb supply.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Positioning">
    In the 2x4 grid of For The King II, place the Busker in the front row if building for Strength/Vitality, or the back row if focusing on Talent and Lutes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Mid-Game Transition">
    By level 4, decide if you are the primary damage dealer or a support. If support, hunt for "Great Lutes" or "Harps" that offer party-wide buffs or healing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Tip">
The Busker's high Strength makes them excellent at using shields. Even a back-row Busker can benefit from a shield if their weapon is one-handed, significantly increasing their survivability.
