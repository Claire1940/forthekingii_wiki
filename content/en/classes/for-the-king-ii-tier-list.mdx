The **Herbalist** is an indispensable asset for any team. Her passive ability to find herbs while traveling reduces your dependency on town shops, while her Party Heal ability keeps the group alive during grueling multi-stage dungeons without burning through valuable Godsbeard. 

The **Alchemist** stands alongside the Herbalist by offering explosive offensive pressure. With the ability to passively generate potions and deploy devastating chemical attacks, the Alchemist can melt high-defense targets and apply pressure to multiple tiles simultaneously.

<Section icon={Shield} title="A-Tier Classes: Reliable Core and Specialist Picks">

A-tier classes are highly dependable and form the foundation of most successful runs. They are straightforward to build, scale exceptionally well with standard gear, and carry specialized utility that keeps the battle grid organized.

The **Blacksmith** is the premier defensive anchor. Using physical armor, shields, and passive abilities like Steadfast and Guard, the Blacksmith absorbs heavy physical attacks that would otherwise crush fragile spellcasters. 

For physical damage, the **Hunter** excels at ranged pressure. Leveraging high Awareness, the Hunter utilizes Elite Ambush and Elite Sneak to control overworld map encounters, while Called Shot rewards perfect dice rolls with devastating critical strikes.

The **Scholar** serves as an excellent alternative magic user, utilizing staves and wands to inflict status effects. His Refocus ability allows him to regain spent Focus at the end of a turn, making him incredibly forgiving for players who need to guarantee important rolls. 

The **Monk** and **Fortune Teller** round out the tier as versatile hybrid choices. The Monk offers secondary healing options, while the Fortune Teller (available in the [Tinkerers of Fate Character Pack](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3840390/For_The_King_II_Tinkerers_Of_Fate_Character_Pack/)) manipulates fate using specialized tarot mechanics.

| Class | Primary Weapon | Best Combat Role | Key Drawback |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Blacksmith | Shield and Hammer | Absorbing frontline pressure | Lower speed delays turns |
| Hunter | Bows, Ranged | Ambushing, single-target DPS | Needs weapon scaling |
| Scholar | Tomes, Staves, Wands | Focus recovery, magic DPS | Low defense, fragile |
| Monk | Hybrid Support | Flexible secondary healing | Overlaps with specialists |
| Fortune Teller | Tarot Cards, Ranged | Tactical fate manipulation | Harder to play than Hunter |

<Callout type="tip" title="Positioning Matters">
Always place your Blacksmith in the front row. The Guard mechanic allows him to redirect damage away from adjacent allies, which is crucial for protecting a back-row Scholar or Herbalist.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="B-Tier Classes: Specialist and DLC Picks">

B-tier classes are not weak, but they require a deeper understanding of the game's mechanics or highly specific team compositions to shine. 

The **Toymaker** is a prime example of a high-ceiling, complex class. Introduced in the [Tinkerers of Fate DLC](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3840390/For_The_King_II_Tinkerers_Of_Fate_Character_Pack/), the Toymaker relies on capturing enemy essence to summon toys onto the battle grid. While these summons can completely reshape combat pressure and distract enemies, the class requires setup turns and careful planning, making it less beginner-friendly than straightforward damage dealers.

| Class | Primary Stat | Best Combat Role | Key Drawback |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Toymaker | Talent | Summon-focused board control | Requires setup turns |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Solo Toymaker Runs">
The Toymaker struggles when left unprotected. If you run a Toymaker, ensure you have a dedicated tank like the Blacksmith to hold the frontline while your summons are being prepared.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Build the Best Party Compositions">

A great party is more than just a collection of highly ranked classes. You must balance your frontline defense, physical damage, magical utility, and healing capabilities to survive the escalating challenges of the campaign.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Your Frontline Defense">
    Select a durable character to occupy the front row. The Blacksmith is the gold standard here, providing high Vitality and shield-block capabilities to protect the rest of your party from direct attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Secure Reliable Group Healing">
    Every party needs a way to recover between fights. The Herbalist is highly recommended for her passive Party Heal ability, ensuring you do not run out of Godsbeard in deep dungeons.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add a High-Value Physical Attacker">
    Bring a character who scales with physical attributes like Strength or Awareness. The Hunter is ideal for this role, providing consistent ranged physical damage and overworld utility like Elite Ambush.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Incorporate Magical Damage or Utility">
    Round out your party with an Intelligence-based damage dealer like the Alchemist or Scholar. This ensures you can bypass high-armor physical enemies using powerful magic attacks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Party Build | Frontline | Damage | Support | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Balanced First Clear | Blacksmith | Hunter, Scholar | Herbalist | Beginner campaign runs |
| High Burst Core | Blacksmith | Hunter, Alchemist | Herbalist | Boss fights, high damage |
| Co-op Duo Split | Blacksmith | Hunter, Scholar | Herbalist | Two-player online co-op |
| Trickster Core | Blacksmith | Toymaker, Fortune Teller | Herbalist | DLC owners, advanced tactics |

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch Your Stat Coverage">
Avoid building a party where everyone shares the same primary stat. If three characters rely on Intelligence, they will constantly fight over the same loot drops, leaving your team undergeared.
</Callout>

</Section>
