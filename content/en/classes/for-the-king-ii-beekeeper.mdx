<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics: Pet Pollinator and Sweet Treats">

The heart of the **For The King II Beekeeper** playstyle lies in its unique interactions with the battlefield. While other classes rely solely on their primary and secondary actions, the Beekeeper extends their influence through the **Pet Pollinator**. This companion acts independently, providing a layer of "free" utility that can turn the tide of a difficult encounter.

**Combat Flow and Grid Dynamics**
On the 2x4 Battle Grid, the Beekeeper should be treated as a high-value utility asset. Because their damage is often split between their own attacks and the pet's actions, keeping them alive is more important than maximizing their individual hit power.

| Mechanic | Effect in Combat | 2026 Meta Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pet Pollinator** | Automatic attacks at start/end of turn | High (Consistent chip damage) |
| **Sweet Treats** | Consumable buffs for the party | Essential for Boss Fights |
| **Sting Attacks** | Damage-over-time (DoT) application | Great against high-HP tanks |
| **Honey Shield** | Temporary protection for allies | Moderate (Situational) |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Placement">
    Place the Beekeeper in the back row, preferably behind a **Blacksmith** or **Fallen Knight**. This ensures they aren't targeted by melee row attacks, allowing the Pet Pollinator to stay active longer.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Applying Stings">
    Use your primary action to apply "Sting" effects to high-priority targets. These effects stack over time, creating a ticking clock for the enemy that doesn't require constant Focus expenditure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Managing Sweet Treats">
    Identify turns where your damage output is secondary to party survival. Use secondary actions to distribute Sweet Treats, which can restore small amounts of HP or provide temporary stat boosts without ending your turn.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Focus Integration">
    Save your **Focus** for the Beekeeper's AOE (Area of Effect) abilities. While the pet handles single targets, the Beekeeper's manual rolls are best used to spread status effects across entire enemy rows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Pet Vulnerability">
In the 2026 update, certain enemies in the **Into The Wild** biomes have "Swatter" traits that specifically target summons. Watch for enemy icons that indicate bonus damage against pets to avoid losing your Pollinator early in the fight.
</Callout>

The **For The King II Beekeeper** shines brightest in the game's most grueling modes. In the **Dark Carnival**, where you face endless floors of escalating difficulty, the Beekeeper's ability to provide damage without consuming limited resources (like herbs or Focus) is a godsend.

**Dark Carnival Tactics**
In the Carnival, the **Wheel of Death** and high-HP bosses can quickly deplete a party's resources. The Beekeeper acts as a "sustain engine." By using the Pet Pollinator to finish off weakened enemies, you save your heavy hitters' Focus for the next floor.

**Dungeon Crawl Gauntlet Tips**
Since the Gauntlet is a seeded challenge, you can predict exactly when the Beekeeper's Sweet Treats will be most effective. 
- **Floor 5-10**: Focus on stacking Stings on the boss while your tank guards the Beekeeper.
- **Floor 15+**: Shift to a full support role, using your secondary actions to keep the party's Focus high through specialized treats.
