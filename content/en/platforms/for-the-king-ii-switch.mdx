<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Roadmap: Surviving Your First Run">

Whether you are streaming the game to a mobile device or playing on a dedicated console, *For The King II* is a challenging turn-based roguelite tabletop RPG that will quickly punish unprepared parties. To make consistent progress and unlock valuable upgrades in the Lore Store, you must master the fundamental rhythm of exploration, town management, and grid-based combat.

Follow this structured roadmap to guide your party safely through the early chapters of the campaign and establish a reliable foundation for future runs.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Build a Balanced Party">
    Do not pick characters based on aesthetic preference alone. A successful party must cover frontline defense, physical damage, magic damage, and support. For beginners, we highly recommend a starting lineup of Blacksmith (Tank), Hunter (Ranged Physical), Scholar (Magic/Control), and Herbalist (Healer).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spend Focus Wisely">
    Focus is your most valuable resource because it guarantees successful dice rolls. Spend Focus to secure high-impact attacks, pass crucial movement checks, or guarantee success on vital town quests. Avoid hoarding Focus until your party is already collapsing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Scout Biomes and Secure Blessings">
    Before rushing toward the main story objective, explore the local biome to locate the Sanctum and the Stone Hero. Sanctums grant powerful passive buffs and provide a life-saving revive safety net, while Stone Heroes grant rapid level boosts and fully restore your Focus.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Treat Towns as Power Spikes">
    Every town you visit is an opportunity to upgrade your gear, replenish your supply of Godsbeard and Panax, or hire a mercenary to absorb frontline pressure. Always check a town's inventory on your first visit and select town quests that offer rewards tailored to your party's current needs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="The Golden Rule of Focus">
Always spend your spare Focus points before interacting with a Stone Hero or a Sanctum. These landmarks fully replenish your Focus meter, so using your remaining points beforehand ensures you get maximum value out of every exploration turn.
</Callout>

</Section>
