<Section icon={Shield} title="Core Combat and Battle Grid Masterclass">

The sequel introduces a revolutionary **2x4 Battle Grid** that completely changes how positioning and combat strategy function compared to the original game. Physical placement on the grid determines which characters are targeted, protected, or hit by area-of-effect (AOE) attacks.

### Grid Positioning Basics
The grid is split into a front row and a back row for each side.

- **Front Row**: Best suited for high-health tanks like the Blacksmith. Frontline characters can use abilities like **Guard** to absorb damage and protect the allies standing directly behind them.
- **Back Row**: The safest place for fragile glass cannons, support healers, and ranged attackers like the Scholar, Herbalist, and Hunter.

| Attack Type | Targeting Grid Pattern | Tactical Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Single Target** | Hits one selected tile | Put high-defense units in the front row |
| **Row Attack** | Hits a horizontal line of tiles | Avoid placing all fragile units in the same row |
| **Column Attack** | Hits a vertical line (front and back) | Do not stack squishy targets directly behind the tank |
| **Splash Attack** | Deals damage to adjacent tiles | Keep distance between key party members |

<Callout type="tip" title="Focus Allocation">
Focus is your most valuable resource. Spend it to guarantee successful dice rolls on critical attacks, heals, or movement. Do not hoard it—Sanctums and Stone Heroes found in the overworld can completely refill your Focus meter, so spend it before visiting them.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Beginner Survival Guide">

Fahrul is beautiful but incredibly deadly. New players often struggle with early run management and the relentless pressure of Chaos. Follow this structured roadmap to secure your first campaign victory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Build a Balanced Party">
    Do not pick four damage dealers. A reliable starter party consists of a **Blacksmith** (frontline tank), a **Hunter** (Awareness physical damage), a **Scholar** (magic damage and Focus recovery), and an **Herbalist** (Group Heal support).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Scout for Sanctums and Stone Heroes">
    Each biome contains a Sanctum and a Stone Hero. Find them before pushing the main quest. Sanctums provide powerful passive buffs and act as a death-preventing safety net, while Stone Heroes grant massive XP boosts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Optimize Town Errands">
    Treat every town as a progression hub. Upon visiting a new town, check for fresh shop inventory, buy up all available **Godsbeard** for healing, and take on low-risk town quests to earn gold and gear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Control Overworld Pulls">
    Before starting a fight on the overworld map, check the red enemy radius. Avoid clumping your party next to multiple roaming mobs, as they will all be pulled into the battle. Use **Ambushes** to isolate single targets.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Loot Distribution Rule">
Always distribute loot based on matching character stats, not arbitrary preference. Give Strength weapons to your Blacksmith, Intelligence tomes to your Scholar, and Awareness bows to your Hunter.
</Callout>

</Section>
